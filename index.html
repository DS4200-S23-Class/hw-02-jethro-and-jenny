High-level task:
  • Consume --> Present: All of the daily maximum temperatures from 1979 - 2021 are laid out in such a way that suggests the creator wants to communicate a message. The title suggests that the visualization is meant to convey the daily maximum temperatures in Vancouver from 1970 to 2021. Hence, the content in this visualization has already been pre-determined and not providing anything new. There also appears to be no visualization that is intended to be stemmed from this one.


    • Medium-level task:
  • Search --> Browse: The locations of points representing certain temperatures is made known by the y-axis, and the locations of points symbolizing particular months is guided by the x-axis. Yet, the target is unknown because there are many points on the visualization the user can observe that all have crucial roles in making the chart effective.


    • Low-level task:
  • Query --> Compare: The creator emphasized the temperatures of the last three days by making them red and labeling them, which makes it seem like they want users to compare those values to the others.


    • Targets:
  • All Data --> Trends: The visualization displays all of the data collected thus far since 1970. Hence, all of the data is vital in conveying the intended message of the plot. The points also form a general trend where August is usually the hottest month of the year.
  • Alternative target: All Data -> Outliers: The visualization draws attention to the daily maximum temperatues in Vancouver in merely the last 3 days compared to all other days in 1979-2021. This strategy is accomplished by how the points representing the last 3 days are red while the others are gray. The three red points indicate that Vancouver experienced abormally higher temperatures during those days in June, as they are located significantly above the cluster of gray points that align neatly together to form a hump. Hence, the three red points are outliers that represent data of interest to the user.
